Privacy Policy

Effective Date: November 26, 2025

Last Updated: November 26, 2025

Ouija Board ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This app is designed for entertainment purposes only and collects minimal data necessary for functionality.

1. Information We Collect

We collect very limited data to improve app stability and user experience:

1.1 Data Collected Automatically

  • Crash Reports: Anonymous crash logs via Firebase Crashlytics to fix bugs
  • Usage Analytics: Anonymous usage statistics via Firebase Analytics (number of sessions, screen views)
  • Device Information: Device model, operating system version (for compatibility)

1.2 Data You Provide

  • Language Preference: Stored locally on your device only
  • App Settings: Vibration and sound preferences (stored locally)

2. Information We DO NOT Collect

We want to be clear about what we don't collect:

  • ❌ Personal information (name, email, phone number)
  • ❌ Location data (GPS coordinates)
  • ❌ Contacts or address book
  • ❌ Photos, videos, or media files
  • ❌ Messages or content you interact with in the app
  • ❌ Ouija board messages or questions
  • ❌ Any personally identifiable information

3. How We Use Your Information

The limited data we collect is used solely to:

  • Fix crashes and technical issues
  • Improve app performance and stability
  • Understand how users interact with the app (which features are used)
  • Ensure compatibility across different devices

We do NOT:

  • Sell your data to third parties
  • Use data for advertising purposes
  • Share data with anyone except Firebase (Google) for analytics

4. Third-Party Services

We use Google Firebase for crash reporting and analytics. Firebase's privacy policy can be found here:

  • Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Google Privacy Policy

Firebase collects data in accordance with their privacy policy. All data transmitted to Firebase is anonymous and cannot be used to identify you personally.

5. Data Security

We take data security seriously:

  • All network communication uses HTTPS encryption
  • We don't store personal data on our servers (because we don't collect it)
  • App settings are stored securely on your device using Android's encrypted DataStore
  • We follow Android security best practices

6. Data Retention

  • Local Data: Settings remain on your device until you uninstall the app
  • Analytics Data: Firebase retains anonymous analytics data according to their retention policy (typically 14 months)
  • Crash Reports: Retained for 90 days to help us fix bugs

7. Children's Privacy

Our app is rated for ages 13 and up. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us immediately.

8. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access: Request what data we have (though we collect minimal data)
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your data
  • Opt-Out: Disable analytics through your device settings
  • Uninstall: Remove the app completely to delete all local data

9. International Users

This app is available worldwide. If you're using it from outside the United States, please be aware that your information may be transferred to and processed in the United States or other countries where Firebase operates.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by:

  • Posting the new Privacy Policy on this page
  • Updating the "Last Updated" date
  • Notifying you in the app (for significant changes)

11. GDPR Compliance (EU Users)

If you are located in the European Union, you have additional rights under GDPR:

  • Right to access your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing

Since we collect minimal anonymous data, most of these rights are automatically satisfied by the nature of our app.

12. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected
  • Know whether personal information is sold or disclosed
  • Opt-out of the sale of personal information
  • Request deletion of personal information

We do not sell personal information.
